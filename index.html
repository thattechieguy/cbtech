What do experts say about DevSecOps?

Industry experts and thought leaders have offered valuable insights into the significance of DevSecOps as a domain. Their sayings highlight the transformative nature of DevSecOps, emphasizing the cultural shift, collaboration, and automation required to build a robust security posture. Let’s delve into six sayings from these experts, each shedding light on different facets of DevSecOps and underscoring its importance in today’s technology landscape.

  • DevSecOps is not just about security; it’s about injecting security DNA into the entire organization and making security a shared responsibility. It involves breaking down silos between development, operations, and security teams, and fostering a culture where security is built-in from the start.

    Gene Kim

    Co-author, The Phoenix Project

  • DevSecOps is the cultural transformation that ensures security is treated as a critical aspect at every stage of the software delivery lifecycle. It emphasizes collaboration between development, operations, and security teams to integrate security practices seamlessly into the development process.

    Shannon Lietz

    Director, Intuit

  • DevSecOps is not a choice anymore; it’s a necessity in the era of frequent cyber threats and evolving compliance requirements. Traditional security approaches that focus on perimeter defense and post-development security assessments are no longer sufficient. DevSecOps brings security into the development process, integrating security practices, and automating security checks from the earliest stages.

    John Willis

    DevOps and IT consultant

  • In DevSecOps, security is everyone’s job, and automation is the key to embedding security practices seamlessly into the development process. By automating security testing, vulnerability scanning, and compliance checks, organizations can ensure consistent and reliable security practices across their software delivery lifecycle.

    Chris Romeo

    CEO, Security Journey

  • DevSecOps is the marriage of development, operations, and security teams to achieve a holistic approach to building secure and reliable software. It’s a mindset shift that emphasizes collaboration, communication, and shared responsibility.

    Jez Humble

    Co-author, Continuous Delivery
